About
Sloka – The Hyderabad Waldorf School is India's pioneering full-fledged Waldorf institution, established in 1997. Rooted in Rudolf Steiner's educational philosophy, the school emphasizes age-appropriate, holistic learning that nurtures the intellectual, emotional, and physical development of each child.
At Sloka, education is a journey of self-discovery, where curiosity is cultivated, and creativity is celebrated. The school fosters a nurturing environment where students are encouraged to explore, question, and engage deeply with their learning experiences. With a curriculum that integrates academics, arts, and practical skills, Sloka aims to develop well-rounded individuals prepared for the complexities of the modern world. The school's commitment to individualized attention ensures that each student's unique talents and interests are recognized and nurtured. Through a blend of traditional wisdom and contemporary insights, Sloka offers an education that is both timeless and relevant.
History
Sloka's inception in 1997 marked a significant milestone as the first comprehensive Waldorf school in India. The journey began with educators and parents seeking an alternative to conventional education, leading to the establishment of a school that harmonizes global Waldorf principles with Indian cultural values. Early initiatives included summer camps and open houses to introduce the community to Waldorf education.
Over the years, Sloka has grown from a modest beginning to a vibrant institution, continually evolving its practices while staying true to its foundational philosophy. The school's commitment to innovation and excellence has positioned it as a leader in alternative education in India. Today, Sloka stands as a testament to the enduring relevance of holistic, child-centered education in the Indian context.
Curriculum
Sloka's curriculum is designed to align with the developmental stages of children, integrating academic rigor with artistic and practical activities. In the early years, storytelling, music, and play lay the foundation for literacy and numeracy. As students progress, subjects like history, science, and mathematics are introduced through experiential learning, fostering deep understanding.
The high school curriculum, affiliated with the CBSE, offers streams in Humanities and Commerce, blending national standards with Waldorf methodologies. Students engage in hands-on projects, arts, and community service, promoting critical thinking and social responsibility. The curriculum also accommodates students with specific learning needs, offering alternative subjects and support mechanisms. Overall, Sloka's educational approach aims to cultivate lifelong learners equipped with the skills and values necessary for personal and societal growth.
Admission
Admissions at Sloka are designed to ensure a good fit between the school's philosophy and the prospective family's expectations. The process begins with an online enquiry form (link here), followed by interactions with the admissions team. Parents are encouraged to visit the campus to experience the environment firsthand. You can contact the Kindergarten & City Office at +91 40 235 452 06, and the Grade School office at +91 741 609 4510.
Eligibility is determined based on age criteria corresponding to each grade level. The school welcomes students from diverse backgrounds, including those with special educational needs, provided the environment can support their growth. For Grades 11 and 12, students can choose between Humanities and Commerce streams, with guidance provided to help them make informed decisions. Throughout the process, transparency and open communication are emphasized to build a strong foundation for the student's educational journey.
