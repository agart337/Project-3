About
Inodai Waldorf School, located in Andheri East, Mumbai, is managed by The Village Foundation (TVF), a non-profit organization. The school is rooted in the principles of Waldorf education, emphasizing holistic development through experiential and age-appropriate learning. Its mission is to awaken the joy of learning by nurturing each child's unique growth and development.
The school fosters a strong connection with nature and promotes inclusivity, ensuring that all children receive quality education. Community involvement is integral, with parents actively participating in school activities and supporting the educational journey. Inodai offers programs from playgroup to Grade 10, integrating academics with arts, crafts, music, and movement to develop well-rounded individuals.
History
Inodai Waldorf School was founded in the year 2011 with a small group of 3 children and 1 teacher. Set up as a single classroom and a small office space offered the initial nurturing environment for this unique system of education.
We are now growing as a school having classes from K.G. upto Grade 10 with over 400 students, 43 teachers and 26 support staff. The 3 pillars of our school are our Children, the Teachers and the Parents. Inodai is a community based school. Parents nurture and sustain the school by familiarising themselves with the philosophy of Waldorf education, contributing their services and expertise and being a support to the class teacher in various ways.
Curriculum
Inodai Waldorf School offers the internationally recognized Steiner Waldorf curriculum, designed to address the developmental stages of children from ages 3 to 18. The curriculum emphasizes a holistic approach, integrating nature, languages, arts, crafts, music, sciences, and mathematics. Early childhood education focuses on imitation and imagination, while the elementary and middle school curriculum includes storytelling, arts, sciences, and physical education. The curriculum aims to develop each child's sense of truth, beauty, and goodness, inspiring a lifelong love of learning.

FAQ
  1. What is Waldorf Education?
    Developed by Rudolf Steiner in 1919, Waldorf Education is based on a developmental approach that addresses the needs of the growing child and maturing adolescent. It aims to educate the whole child—the heart and the hands, as well as the head.
  2. What is the curriculum like in a Waldorf school?
    The curriculum is designed to meet various stages of child development, fostering enthusiasm for learning. Preschool and Kindergarten focus on imitation and imagination, while elementary and middle school include subjects like literature, history, sciences, mathematics, foreign languages, arts, and physical education.
  3. How does Inodai Waldorf School involve parents?
    Inodai is a community-based school where parents play an active role by familiarizing themselves with Waldorf philosophy, contributing their services and expertise, and supporting class teachers in various ways.
