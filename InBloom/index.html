About
InBloom is a Waldorf-inspired kindergarten and daycare nestled within a serene 2-acre park on Old Airport Road. Rooted in Rudolf Steiner’s educational philosophy, the school emphasizes nurturing universal human values through meaningful learning experiences. The environment is designed to be unhurried and secure, allowing children to explore and experience the richness of childhood.
At InBloom, children engage in creative play and real-world activities like cooking, gardening, and storytelling, fostering holistic development. The faculty comprises trained early childhood educators who undergo regular training in child-centric Waldorf practices. InBloom is recognized by the International Association of Steiner/Waldorf Early Childhood Education (IASWECE) and is a proud member of the Sadhana Indian Waldorf Kindergarten Association (SWIKA).
History
Founded in 2004, InBloom has been dedicated to helping children truly bloom through the Waldorf approach. The school was established by Pooja Marshall, a trained Waldorf teacher with over two decades of experience in early childhood education, and Ryan Marshall, a former professional jockey with a deep understanding of nurturing environments.
Their combined expertise has shaped InBloom into a space where children can develop physically, emotionally, and cognitively. Over the years, InBloom has expanded its programs to include parent-toddler groups, kindergarten, after-school care, and a homeschool program, all tailored to the needs of young learners. The school's commitment to the Waldorf philosophy has made it a cornerstone in the Indian Waldorf education movement.
Curriculum
InBloom's curriculum is experiential and multi-sensory, designed to align with the natural rhythms of the day, seasons, and festivals. Rather than teaching numbers, language, and science in isolation, these subjects are interwoven into daily activities like circle time, storytelling, cooking, and nature walks.
Children develop numeracy through rhymes and domestic tasks, language through rich vocabulary in songs and stories, and scientific understanding through gardening and observing seasonal changes. The curriculum emphasizes the development of life skills, values, and habits that last a lifetime, fostering a lifelong love for learning. ​
Admission
To experience the InBloom way, prospective parents are invited to schedule a school visit by calling +91 98454 91707 or requesting one through this portal. During the visit, families can tour the school, meet the faculty, and learn about the customized curriculum tailored to each child's needs.
Following the visit, parents can submit an application and pay the fees to enroll their child. A home visit is then scheduled, allowing InBloom teachers to understand the child's home environment better. Finally, parents are invited to observe a class, gaining deeper insight into the school's approach to early childhood education. ​
FAQ
  1. What is a day at InBloom like?
    A typical day at InBloom provides a peaceful and predictable environment where children feel safe to explore. Activities include creative play, cooking, painting, crafts, gardening, singing, storytelling, and daily nature walks. The day follows a reassuring pattern, balancing child-led and teacher-led activities.
  2. How will my child learn numbers, language, and science at InBloom?
    These subjects are integrated into daily activities. Numeracy is developed through rhymes and domestic tasks; language through songs and stories; science through gardening and observing seasonal changes.​
  3. How is my child’s progress evaluated?
    Teachers mindfully observe each child's development, collaborating with parents to build on their progress both at school and home. Aside from this, children engage in meaningful real work like painting, cooking, gardening, and crafts, developing motor skills, social skills, and a sense of self.​
