About
Nagpur Waldorf Inspired School (NWIS) is dedicated to fostering holistic development in children through the principles of Waldorf education. Emphasizing real-world experiences over virtual ones, NWIS nurtures the intellectual, emotional, and creative facets of each child. The school believes in unhurried learning, allowing children to explore and grow at their own pace.
By integrating arts, nature, and hands-on activities into the curriculum, NWIS creates an environment where imagination and discovery thrive. The school's philosophy centers on cultivating a lifelong love for learning, ensuring that students are well-prepared for the dynamic world ahead. With a focus on the developmental stages of childhood, NWIS provides a nurturing space where children can flourish.
History
Established under the guidance of the CDS Group, NWIS was founded to bring the Waldorf educational approach to Nagpur. Recognizing the need for an education system that values the holistic growth of children, the school embarked on a journey to integrate the Waldorf philosophy into its teaching
Over the years, NWIS has expanded its programs, starting from Nursery and Kindergarten to offering education up to the seventh grade. The school's commitment to unhurried and meaningful learning experiences has been the cornerstone of its growth. By staying true to the principles of Waldorf education, NWIS has established itself as a beacon for holistic learning in the region. The school's history is a testament to its dedication to nurturing well-rounded individuals.
Curriculum
The curriculum at NWIS is inspired by the Steiner Waldorf approach, emphasizing the harmonious development of the head, heart, and hands. Academic subjects are interwoven with arts, crafts, music, and nature-based activities, ensuring a well-rounded education.
The school offers programs from Nursery to Grade 7, with a focus on age-appropriate learning that aligns with the developmental stages of children. Beyond Grade 7, students transition to the ICSE-affiliated CDS School at Katol Road to complete their high school education. The curriculum is designed to foster creativity, critical thinking, and emotional intelligence, preparing students for the challenges of the modern world. By integrating experiential learning with academic rigor, NWIS ensures that students develop a deep understanding and appreciation for their studies.​
Admission
Prospective parents interested in enrolling their children at NWIS can initiate the admission process by contacting the school directly. The school encourages parents to schedule a visit to understand the Waldorf-inspired environment and teaching methodologies. During the admission process, emphasis is placed on ensuring that the school's philosophy aligns with the family's educational values.
Admissions are open for Nursery to Grade 7, with the school providing guidance on the transition to the ICSE curriculum for higher grades. FAQ
Browse below to see our most frequently asked questions. Click here for answers to these questions.
  1. Why choose a Waldorf school for my child?
    Waldorf schools prioritize the holistic development of children, focusing on intellectual, emotional, and creative growth. They provide nurturing environments that respect the wonder of childhood, integrating play, nature, and hands-on learning into the curriculum.
  2. Why is formal reading introduced later in Waldorf schools?
    Formal reading is typically introduced in Grade 1 to align with children's developmental readiness. This approach allows children to fully engage in imaginative play and experiential learning during their early years, laying a strong foundation for future academic skills.
  3. Why do Waldorf schools discourage TV watching and electronic media?
    Electronic media are believed by Waldorf teachers to seriously hamper the development of the child’s imagination – a faculty which is believed to be central to the healthy development of the individual. Computer use by young children is also discouraged.
  4. How does grading work
    A full assessment of each student’s progress is provided in the form of a year-end narrative assessment in all subject areas. These assessments are supported by teacher conferences and class meetings throughout the year.
  5. Does Waldorf education suit all children?
    Our education suits a wide range of children and is helpful to children who enjoy insightful teachers and a warm and friendly environment. The confidence and support of parents who have a good understanding of the schools ’aims is very helpful to their children.
