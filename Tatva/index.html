About
Tatva School, located in Polachery, Chennai, is a Waldorf-inspired institution dedicated to holistic education. Emphasizing the development of the head, heart, and hands, the school fosters an environment where children engage deeply with age-appropriate challenges and experiences. The curriculum is designed to instill a love for learning, focusing on timeless knowledge, skills, values, and attitudes. Tatva aims to nurture each child's potential, preparing them to thrive amidst change as lifelong learners.
History
Founded in 2009 by S. Balasubramanian and R. Vidyalakshmi, both alumni of IIM Ahmedabad, Tatva School began as a preschool in Velachery. Recognizing the need for a transformative approach to education, they established Tatva to prioritize the child's developmental journey.
Over the years, the school expanded, now operating campuses in Velachery and Medavakkam, with plans to upgrade to a full-fledged K-12 institution. Tatva's mission is to lead a positive transformation in education, making learning a joyful and purposeful experience.
Curriculum
Kindergarten (Nursery to 6 years)
Indradhanu's Early Childhood Programme, encompassing Nursery (24 months to 3½ years) and Kindergarten (3½ to 6 years), is designed as a home away from home. Recognizing that these formative years are crucial, the school emphasizes learning through imitation and repetition, seamlessly integrating education into meaningful work within a nurturing environment.
Primary Grades (Grades 1–7)
In the Grade School, each day begins with a two-hour Main Lesson focusing on a single subject for four weeks, utilizing movement, art, music, and storytelling. Subjects are taught through hands-on experiences, ensuring that education engages all senses and fosters a deep connection to the material.
High School (Grades 8–12)
Starting from Grade 8, Indradhanu Waldorf School transitions to the Cambridge International curriculum, preparing students for the IGCSE examinations in Grade 10. Despite this shift, the school continues to employ Waldorf principles, ensuring a stress-free environment that encourages experimentation, real-world application through camps and outdoor trips, and the cultivation of healthy social relationships and self-awareness.
Admission
Admissions at Tatva School are open for the academic year 2025-26. Children must be at least 6 years old before the start of Grade 1, in line with the National Education Policy (NEP) guidelines. The admission process involves submitting an enquiry form, followed by an interaction session to assess the child's readiness and alignment with the school's philosophy.
Required documents include the child's birth certificate, photographs, and previous academic records if applicable. The school management reserves the right to make the final decision on class placement. You can access the admission enquiry form through this link.
