About
Bangalore Steiner School is an independent, co-educational institution rooted in the principles of Rudolf Steiner's Waldorf education. Recognized by the Government of Karnataka, the school operates as a not-for-profit organization. Its curriculum, tailored for the Indian context, emphasizes the harmonious development of the head, heart, and hands, fostering intellectual, emotional, and practical growth.
A collaborative community of teachers and parents works together to nurture a sustainable and holistic learning environment. The school's ethos is encapsulated in its guiding principle: "Our highest endeavor must be to develop free human beings who are able out of their own initiative to impart purpose and direction to their lives."
History
The inception of Bangalore Steiner School was driven by a collective of parents seeking an educational approach that prioritized holistic child development over mere academic achievement. Dissatisfied with conventional schooling methods, they were drawn to the Waldorf philosophy, which resonated with their aspirations for their children's growth.
This philosophy emphasizes age-appropriate learning experiences, nurturing curiosity, and fostering empathy and self-reliance. The school's foundation reflects a commitment to creating an environment where children's physical, emotional, and intellectual needs are met in harmony. ​
Curriculum
The school's curriculum is structured around the developmental stages of children:​
Kindergarten (Ages 3.5–7): Focuses on physical growth through activities emphasizing rhythm, repetition, and reverence. Early academics are intentionally deferred to support natural development.
Grade School (Ages 7–14): Subjects like English, Mathematics, Science, and Languages are taught using creative and artistic methods in thematic blocks, facilitating deep and immersive learning experiences.
High School (Ages 14–18): Offers the Cambridge IGCSE and AS/A Level programs, integrating rigorous academics with non-Cambridge components like arts, internships, and community engagement to support holistic adolescent development. ​
Admission
In the interest of maintaining a distraction-free environment on school days, we do not entertain one-on-one visits for exploring the school for admissions.

Prospective parents are requested to follow the process mentioned above:
  1. Please avoid phone calls regarding admissions.
  2. Please click the registration button at the bottom of this page and fill in the form. You will receive a response in 3 working days.
  3. If you do not hear back from us, please send a message to admissions@bangaloresteinerschool.org to follow up.
  4. Kindly ensure that you read ALL the information on our website thoroughly to gauge if Steiner education could be the path for you as a parent.
Prospective parents are required to attend an orientation session that provides an overview of Steiner education principles and the school's approach to child development. This session includes classroom observations, curriculum insights, and discussions on the school's history and management. Attendance is essential for making an informed decision about enrollment. Notably, children are not permitted to attend this session, except when applying for high school grades.
FAQ
  1. What educational philosophy does the school follow?
    The school adheres to Rudolf Steiner's Waldorf education principles, emphasizing holistic development.
  2. Are early academics introduced in kindergarten?
    No, early academics are deferred to focus on physical growth and experiential learning.
  3. What curriculum is followed in high school?
    The school offers Cambridge IGCSE for Grades 9–10 and AS/A Levels for Grades 11–12.
  4. How can parents get involved?
    Parents are encouraged to participate in school activities and events, fostering a strong community bond. ​
