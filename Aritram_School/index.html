About
Aritram School is a Waldorf-inspired educational institution located in Noida, India. It offers a holistic approach to education, nurturing the physical, emotional, intellectual, social, and spiritual development of children. The school provides a nurturing, joyful, and media-free environment with a dynamic curriculum that integrates arts into all academic disciplines. Aritram is a strong community of families who have come together to establish the school and are actively engaged in its operations.
History
Aritram School was established by a community of families seeking a holistic and artistic approach to education for their children. Inspired by the Waldorf philosophy, which originated from the work of Rudolf Steiner in 1919, the school aims to provide an education that addresses the needs of the whole child.
Since its inception, Aritram has grown into a nurturing space that emphasizes experiential learning and the integration of arts across the curriculum. The school's development reflects a commitment to creating an environment where children can thrive intellectually, emotionally, and socially.
Curriculum
Aritram's curriculum is designed to align with the developmental stages of children, offering programs from Kindergarten (ages 2.5–6) through Grade 6. In Kindergarten, children engage in activities like free play, cooking, crafting, and gardening, fostering learning through imitation and daily rhythms.
From Grade 1 onwards, the curriculum includes language arts, mathematics, science, form drawing, foreign languages, handwork, and sports, all taught creatively through stories, poetry, and movement. The curriculum emphasizes experiential learning, integrating arts into all subjects to cultivate clear thinking and a lifelong love of learning.
Admission
Aritram School's admission process begins with an Orientation/School Visit, where prospective parents can learn about the Waldorf education approach and the school's environment. Following the orientation, parents interested in enrolling their child can submit an admission form. The next step involves a Child Observation period, lasting 1 to 5 days, to assess the child's readiness and compatibility with the school's approach. After the observation, the school communicates the outcome within 48 hours. Upon acceptance, an Induction session is conducted to guide parents through the school's practices and help them integrate into the Aritram community.
