About
Uday, meaning "sunrise," is Jaipur's first and only school inspired by the Waldorf philosophy. Established in November 2014, it began as a Kindergarten with just four children. Founded by Gurpreet, a mother seeking holistic education for her child, Uday emphasizes experiential learning and the development of critical thinking, emotional intelligence, and artistic expression. The school aims to nurture self-motivated and responsible individuals, preparing them to make positive contributions to society.
History
Uday's inception was driven by Gurpreet's quest for meaningful education aligning with her values. Discovering the Waldorf philosophy, which emphasizes holistic child development, she transitioned from her corporate career to establish Uday in 2014. Starting with a small Kindergarten, the school has grown steadily, now offering classes up to Grade 5, with plans to expand to Grade 12. Uday remains committed to quality education, ensuring each child's growth is nurtured without compromising its core ethos.
Curriculum
Uday's curriculum is rooted in Waldorf education principles, focusing on holistic development—intellectual, emotional, and physical. It integrates arts and academics, promoting age-appropriate, joyous, and self-directed learning. The approach emphasizes imagination over rote learning, fostering creativity and resilience. By catering to the developmental stages of children, Uday aims to instill a lifelong love for learning and prepare students to face future challenges with confidence.
Admission
Admissions at Uday are open throughout the year, subject to seat availability. The process begins with an Orientation Programme for prospective parents (click here to regsiter for orientation), followed by the collection of admission forms. Subsequent meetings with parents and the child are scheduled to ensure alignment with the school's philosophy. Uday emphasizes a collaborative partnership with families, encouraging active parental involvement in the child's educational journey.

FAQ
  1. What are the school timings?
    Timings vary by age group, ranging from 2 to 4.5 hours for younger children, gradually increasing as the child settles.
  2. Can parents stay with the child during school hours?
    While initial transitions are supported, parents are encouraged not to stay to help the child adapt independently.
  3. Is there a school uniform?
    No uniform is required. Children should wear comfortable, simple clothing suitable for play.
