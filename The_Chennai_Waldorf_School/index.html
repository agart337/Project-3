About
The Chennai Waldorf School, also known as Akshar Centre of Education, embraces the philosophy of integral education inspired by Sri Aurobindo and The Mother. This approach emphasizes nurturing the physical, emotional, volitional, mental, and spiritual aspects of a child. The school believes in evoking knowledge from within each student, rather than merely imparting it.
Teachers act as guides, facilitating an environment where children can explore and discover their innate potentials. The curriculum is designed to stimulate inner activity, encouraging students to engage deeply with their learning experiences. This holistic methodology aims to develop well-rounded individuals who are self-aware and capable of independent thought.
History
Waldorf Education was conceived by Austrian philosopher Rudolf Steiner in 1919, aiming to address the comprehensive development of children. The first Waldorf School was established in Stuttgart, Germany, in collaboration with Emil Molt, with the vision of cultivating clear-thinking, compassionate, and conscientious individuals. This educational model emphasizes understanding human development and tailoring teaching methods accordingly.
The Chennai Waldorf School integrates this philosophy, adapting it to the Indian context while maintaining the core principles of Steiner's vision. Over the years, the school has evolved, blending traditional Indian educational values with the Waldorf methodology to create a unique learning environment.
Curriculum
The Chennai Waldorf School offers a comprehensive curriculum that caters to various stages of child development:
Early Childhood (KG 1-3)
Focuses on the pillars of goodness, beauty, and truth. Activities include yoga, nature studies, music, movement, and exposure to multiple languages like Sanskrit, Tamil, Hindi, English, and French.
Lower Grades (1-5)
Emphasizes storytelling, arts, and hands-on activities. Subjects like ancient history, botany, and geography are introduced through engaging methods.
Middle School (6-8)
Students delve into subjects like the Renaissance, mechanics, and human physiology. The curriculum supports their growing independence and critical thinking skills.
High School (9-10)
Offers a choice between the Waldorf curriculum and the IGCSE program, preparing students for higher education and global opportunities.

FAQ
  1. What is unique about AKSHAR? How is it different from other traditional schools?
    The aim is to educate the whole child, “head, heart and hands”. The curriculum is as broad as time will allow and balances academic subjects with artistic and practical activities.
  2. How long has AKSHAR been in existence?
    AKSHAR was instituted in 2011 and every year the class will be upgraded till Grade 12.
  3. When do children begin formal learning?
    Children start formal learning (i.e.) reading, writing and numeracy in grade one at the age of 6. Cognitive skills can be introduced with relative ease if children have first had the opportunity to develop speech, co-ordination and their relationship to themselves, others and the world around them during the early years (KG).
  4. What role does art play in the curriculum?
    Art is a keystone of the Waldorf curriculum, with artistic activities woven into the entire curriculum. Art is not a compartmentalized “lesson” presented without any relationship to the rest of the curriculum. It is an element not only of every activity in the curriculum, but even of every moment spent in the school experience.
