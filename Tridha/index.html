About
Think education only happens through memorization and testing? Think again. At Tridha, we add music and art to science, math and English to help our students reach a deeper understanding of their relative concepts.
Like all Steiner schools, Tridha is a secular co-educational and non-selective. Admission is strictly on a first-come first-served basis after meeting with the child and the parents though we give preference to those who have attended our Steiner Education Course. We accept admission forms throughout the year. Boys and girls wear the same uniform – a short cotton kurta combined with their choice of trousers. All Tridha students perform on stage; practise handwork, dance and music as well as drawing and painting; develop skills at cooking, carpentry and gardening while mastering the curriculum till class 12.
History
Tridha follows Rudolf Steiner's educational philosophy, designing its curriculum around a deep understanding of each child’s nature and encouraging independent thinking through meaningful life experiences. This approach nurtures a child’s sense of wonder, imagination, and curiosity about the world. While the school is inspired by Steiner's educational ideas, it does not align itself with the Anthroposophical Society.
However, Tridha remains actively connected to the broader Steiner School community. The school engages in sharing knowledge, student exchanges, and hosting experienced visiting teachers to stay updated with global Steiner practices. Tridha aims to offer a holistic education that fosters not just academic skills, but also emotional and creative growth. The ultimate goal is to prepare students to become self-disciplined, socially aware, and inwardly free adults.
Curriculum
Balance is the key - fostered by small class sizes, enthusiastic teachers who truly endeavour to know each student, a holistic variety of challenging opportunities. Everything we do at Tridha works in harmony so children can tap their talents, follow their passions and make a difference in the world.
Respecting the basics
Students are grounded in essential academic subjects like history, science, and mathematics, while also exploring unique, non-mandated disciplines like drama and the history of poetry and architecture, enriching their educational experience.
Subjects
Learning is experiential, with tactile and artistic methods—like painting, drama, and music—used to build academic understanding and critical thinking, tailored to each developmental stage.
Eurythmy
Eurythmy translates the sounds, phrases and rhythms of speech, or the dynamic elements of music into movement and gesture. The result has been described as visible speech and visible music. In the art of eurythmy, that which you would normally hear, you see in the movements of the eurythmist. Through eurythmy the children learn to listen carefully and to express, through appropriate movement, what they hear.
Music
Music is embedded throughout the grades, beginning with singing and the recorder in early years, and expanding to instruments like Alto, Tenor, percussion, and strings as students grow.
Fine Arts and Handwork
Artistic and manual activities—drawing, knitting, clay work, and woodworking—encourage creativity, precision, and the development of fine motor skills.

Admission process
  1. Admissions are on first come first basis, except for siblings, teachers' children, students from other Steiner schools (outside of Maharashtra applying for Grade 1 upwards) and those who have attended the Steiner Education Course.
  2. Forms are accepted throughout the year on school working Saturdays between 9.00 am to 12.00 noon.
  3. Register yourself for an orientation program, which happen couple of times a year. It is compulsory for both the parents to attend the orientation.
  4. Once you have submitted the form, you will be called for a group meeting if there is a vacancy.
  5. At the group meeting an admission registration fee of Rs 2000/- will have to be paid.
  6. The parents and children will next have to meet the admission committee. Decision for acceptance is made by the Admission team.
  7. You get a call within a week's time about the decision.
  8. Within a week's time the fee payment has to be made after the call and the admission is confirmed.
FAQ
Browse below to see our most frequently asked questions. Click here for answers to these questions.

  1. Why do we teach reading so late?
    Late readers are not at a disadvantage; they often develop a deeper, lifelong interest in reading. Reading is introduced when the child is naturally ready, avoiding stress and fostering genuine curiosity.
  2. Tell me about the Main Lesson?
    Main Lesson is a core feature of the Steiner curriculum, where students focus on one integrated topic each morning for 3–4 weeks using storytelling, art, music, and movement to deeply engage with the subject. Each lesson is tailored to the child’s developmental stage.
  3. Why do Tridha recommend the limiting of television, videos, and radio for young children?
    Limiting media helps protect and stimulate the child's imagination, which is essential to Steiner education. Concerns include not only content but also the physical and developmental impact of screen exposure.
  4. How can a class teacher teach all the subjects through the years of elementary schooling?
    The class teacher leads the daily Main Lesson and forms a deep, lasting relationship with students, fostering academic and personal growth. Specialist teachers support in areas like music and languages, but the class teacher guides the overall learning journey.
