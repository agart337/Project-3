About
Aarambh Waldorf School, situated in Vasant Kunj, New Delhi, offers education from Kindergarten through Grade 5. Rooted in the Waldorf philosophy developed by Rudolf Steiner, the school emphasizes holistic development—nurturing the head (intellect), heart (emotions), and hands (practical skills).
Aarambh's approach integrates academic learning with physical activity, arts, and moral responsibility, fostering a balanced growth of body, mind, and spirit. The school also emphasizes a strong connection to nature and the local culture, integrating seasonal festivals and environmental stewardship into the learning experience.
History
Established in 2013, Aarambh began as a small kindergarten with just four children. Over time, it expanded to include primary grades up to Grade 5, reflecting a growing interest in alternative education in Delhi. In its early years, the school moved to a more spacious campus at Shanti Kunj Farms to accommodate its expanding community.
The journey has been marked by active parent participation, from setting up classrooms to organizing events that promote the Waldorf philosophy. Workshops and orientations have been integral in building awareness about the school's unique approach to education.
Curriculum
Aarambh's curriculum is age-appropriate and focuses on the physical, emotional, and intellectual development of children. In the early years, learning is centered around imitation and play, with activities like unstructured play, arts, gardening, and cooking to develop foundational senses and motor skills.
As children progress to primary grades, the curriculum integrates academic subjects with arts, music, and movement, fostering a love for learning.
The school does not employ traditional grading systems; instead, teachers maintain detailed narrative reports that provide a comprehensive view of each child's progress. This approach ensures that education is tailored to meet the individual needs of each student.

FAQ
  1. How many children are there in each class?
    Kindergarten groups typically consist of 15-20 children with two teachers and additional staff. Primary grade classes are smaller, ranging from 4-8 students.
  2. What do kindergarten children do if there is no formal academics?
    The kindergarten curriculum focuses on unstructured play, arts, gardening, and other hands-on activities that build foundational skills and prepare children for academic learning.
  3. How do children transition to formal academics later on?
    The foundation built through early activities ensures that children develop the necessary skills for academic learning, such as focus, motor skills, and the ability to sit for longer durations.
  4. How is reading taught at Aarambh?
    Reading and writing are introduced after the age of six, aligning with the child's developmental readiness.
