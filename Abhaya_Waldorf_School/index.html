About
Abhaya School is a Waldorf institution dedicated to nurturing holistic development in children. Rooted in the educational philosophies of Rudolf Steiner, the school emphasizes creativity, critical thinking, and emotional intelligence. By integrating arts, sciences, and humanities, Abhaya fosters an environment where students can explore and grow at their own pace.
The curriculum is designed to align with the natural developmental stages of children, ensuring age-appropriate learning experiences. Abhaya's commitment to individualized education aims to cultivate a lifelong love for learning. The school community values collaboration among teachers, parents, and students, creating a supportive and enriching atmosphere.​
History
Founded by a group of concerned parents and educators, Abhaya School emerged from a desire to offer an alternative to conventional education systems. Drawing inspiration from the Waldorf curriculum, the founders sought to create a learning environment that places the child's growth at the center.
The school's inception was driven by questions about the nature of education and a commitment to fostering creativity and freedom in learning. Over the years, Abhaya has evolved, continually adapting to meet the needs of its students while staying true to its foundational principles. The school's journey reflects a dedication to educational innovation and holistic development. Through community engagement and reflective practices, Abhaya continues to refine its approach to education.
Curriculum
Abhaya School's curriculum is grounded in the Waldorf or Steiner educational philosophy, emphasizing age-appropriate, holistic learning. The program integrates arts, sciences, and humanities, fostering creativity and critical thinking. In early childhood, the focus is on imaginative play and artistic expression, laying the foundation for cognitive development.
As students progress, the curriculum evolves to include more structured academic content, aligned with their developmental stages. The high school program incorporates the CBSE curriculum, ensuring students are well-prepared for standardized assessments. Throughout all levels, the emphasis remains on nurturing the whole child—intellectually, emotionally, and physically. This approach aims to cultivate independent thinkers who are socially responsible and creatively engaged.​
Admission
The admission process at Abhaya School begins with submitting an application form, available on the school's website. Prospective families are then invited to visit the campus, providing an opportunity to experience the school's environment firsthand. During the visit, parents can engage with faculty members to discuss the school's philosophy and curriculum.
An admission form is to be filled out during this visit, accompanied by the payment of tuition fees. The school encourages open communication to ensure alignment between the family's educational values and Abhaya's approach. This process is designed to foster a mutual understanding and commitment to the child's holistic development. For detailed information, interested parties should refer to the school's admissions page.
FAQ
  1. What educational philosophy does Abhaya follow?
    Abhaya adheres to the Waldorf or Steiner educational approach, focusing on holistic, age-appropriate learning.
  2. How does the curriculum evolve across grade levels?
    The curriculum transitions from imaginative play in early childhood to structured academics in higher grades, incorporating the CBSE curriculum in high school.​
  3. What is the teacher-student ratio?
    Abhaya emphasizes individualized attention, maintaining low teacher-student ratios to support personalized learning.
  4. Are extracurricular activities offered?
    Yes, the school integrates arts, music, and physical education into the curriculum, promoting well-rounded development.
  5. How can I schedule a visit?
    Prospective families can schedule a campus visit through the admissions section on the school's website.​
