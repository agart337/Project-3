About
Kingdom of Childhood (KOC) is a Steiner-Waldorf school located in Bangalore, India. Established in 2007, the school is grounded in the educational philosophy of Dr. Rudolf Steiner, emphasizing holistic development. KOC fosters an inclusive environment that respects diverse abilities, aiming to nurture creativity, critical thinking, and a deep connection to the world. The school integrates academics with arts, music, movement, and practical activities, promoting hands-on learning experiences. Through this approach, KOC aspires to cultivate well-rounded individuals prepared to meet the challenges of the world.
History
Founded in 2007, Kingdom of Childhood is one of the oldest Waldorf-Steiner schools in Bangalore. The school was established by Mr. Manivannan, an educator with over 30 years of experience, who initiated the institution based on his personal experience of homeschooling his children during a time when the concept was not widely recognized in India.
His vision was to create an educational environment rooted in Steiner's philosophy, focusing on holistic development and inclusivity. Over the years, KOC has grown into a vibrant community that continues to uphold these foundational principles.
Curriculum
The curriculum at Kingdom of Childhood is designed to address the holistic development of students, integrating academics with artistic and practical activities. Subjects include literacy, numeracy, sciences, history, geography, music, fine arts, and practical skills.
The program is structured into stages: Veda Kindergarten for early childhood, Upanishad for middle school, and Kalpataru for higher grades. Each stage is designed to align with the developmental needs of students, fostering creativity, critical thinking, and emotional intelligence. Physical education, including activities like sports and the Olympics, is an integral part of the curriculum, promoting physical well-being and teamwork.
The school also celebrates various festivals and cultural events, enriching the students' social and cultural understanding. By providing a comprehensive and balanced education, the Kingdom of Childhood aims to cultivate well-rounded individuals equipped for lifelong learning.
Admission
The admission process at Kingdom of Childhood begins with parents or guardians attending an Open House session, where they can interact with teachers and gain insight into the school's philosophy and environment. Following this, interested parties are encouraged to express their intent to proceed with the admission process by -
STEP 1

Call (+91 86181 01931)/ text on WhatsApp for basic details or send an email to enquiry.koc@gmail.com. Include your child’s name, date of birth, name of the current / previous school and reason for change, etc. Also please mention whether you are seeking admission or exploring possibilities and send it along with your name, mobile number (preferably WhatsApp number) and your current location. Our team will respond to you in a day or two.
STEP 2
It is mandatory for both parents to attend this session. It is a session conducted for parents seeking admission with us, to provide an understanding of the “Why”, “How” and “What” of Waldorf Education.
  1. Our Founder, Mr. Manivannan explains the basics of the Waldorf philosophy and the developmental stages of a child
  2. Our Teachers present the Waldorf curriculum as it unfolds through our grade classes
  3. An exhibition of our children’s books, handwork, paintings etc., gives prospective parents a peek into how this curriculum gets executed in the classroom
  4. An explanation of our ethos, along with logistics such as fee structure, transport, school lunch etc., is the concluding part of this session
  5. This session will help each parent determine if they truly desire Waldorf education for their child and the commitment it will take (as a parent) in their child’s journey
STEP 3
After attending the Open House session, send email to: to express their interest in taking forward the admission process for their child.
STEP 4
Assessment of the child– this will be done as a physical meeting for 4-5 days in the campus the child is being considered for. The purpose of this meeting will be to observe the child and determine the child’s needs and assess if we are able to meet the child’s needs. This will also be an opportunity for us to interact with the parents and explain the workings of our school. The parents also will get an opportunity to take a tour of our campus.
STEP 5
Application – Once it is determined that parents have got all the relevant information about the school, they resonate with Steiner philosophy and are willing to be a part of a community that is committed to learning and growing together, they are sent an Application form. The submission of the completed form along with relevant documents and payment of all fees will conclude the admission process.
